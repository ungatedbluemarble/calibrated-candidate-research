Calibrated Candidate Research

What the newest research on AI hiring actually proves, and the few moves it shows make a difference for the people sending out resumes.

Two pieces A short read and a technical analysis

Roughly nine in ten U.S. employers now screen applicants with AI, most of it bought from the same handful of vendors. Two recent studies, one a deployment-scale analysis from Stanford, Chapman, and Northeastern, the other a controlled experiment on large language models, show what that concentration does to job seekers and where a candidate has real, evidence-backed room to respond. These two pieces work through the findings and the defensible response.

The throughline

A single vendor’s model can reject the same person everywhere it is used, and current LLM screeners reward materials that read the way they expect. Calibrated Candidate works the three levers the research supports: breadth across distinct screening systems, per-position tailoring, and AI-shaped materials. The benefit is bounded and honest: it improves how an applicant meets the screening layer, not whether they get hired.
